Statistics on Savings Earned

TREES SAVED

The global demand for paper continues to rise steadily, leading to millions of hectares of forests being harvested each year, despite efforts in recycling. Surprisingly, there are still companies and institutions that print out even their email communications. Projections indicate that by 2060, the worldwide demand for paper and pulp will double be compared to 2010 levels.

However, it's crucial to recognize that everyone has the power to make a positive impact by reducing their paper usage. This includes businesses transitioning from paper-based workflows to electronic processes made possible by advanced technology.

According to a Survey, switching to a paperless office saves 48000 trees in a year which indicates we save 131 trees in a day
This shift is not only anticipated by consumers who increasingly prefer online payments and electronic document signing but also brings benefits like environmental preservation, substantial cost savings, and enhanced competitiveness.

TIME SAVED

Time is money!
Each day, employees spend up to two hours looking for files. The costs start to mount when you look at the figures over the course of a whole year.

Large enterprises lose a file every six seconds on average. Losing a file might result in serious security risks and the possibility of legal action. It's crucial to keep vital documents organized and stored in a secure manner. Furthermore, managers may spend an average of 4 weeks each year looking for lost or missing information. This crucial time was lost as a result of inadequate file management.

A paperless record-management system eliminates about 4 days of file processing time. When it comes to time savings, this is a huge advancement. Saving time for personnel within the organization is made possible through the scanning of records. Time savings create opportunity for greater personal development and project development.
